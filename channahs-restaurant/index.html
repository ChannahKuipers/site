Channah’s Restaurant

This restaurant website is inspired by the work of graphic designer Gerard Unger. The graphical elements on the website are clearly influenced by this artwork.

Visual research

For my research I mainly looked at the shapes of the different fonts
by Gerard Unger. Because he was mainly a typographer, there was not much design I could find online, but he has published several typography books in which I got inspiration from his book covers. I also found a vew images online that matched his work and were I can also draw my inspiration from.

Shape research

Because, Unger was mainly a typographer, I got most of my inspiration from the shapes of the letters and the covers from the typography books he published.

Icon sketches

I have tried to make as many different skectches as possible, paying particular attention to the variations of shapes. Beside are some sketches of the icons.

Fonts and colors

I saw the color blue (almost purple) most often in his designs
as a base color, which is why I also used this color as the main color/base in my design. In addition, I often saw that he also used a striking color next to it for the shapes, I also have applied this in my design choice.

The type fonts I used are from Gerard Unger himself. If you pay attention to the details of the fonts, they are quite different from each other. In my design I wanted the same effect also to come back.

Design Choices

The designs of Gerard Unger are quite simple but also very playful, because of the different shapes and compositions he experimented with. For my design I have used details of his font letters, also positioned in a similar way like Gerard Unger.
Because the title and text often do not stand out in his designs (bookcovers) I have not displayed these very conspicuously either, the attention is mainly focused on the letters and how they are placed. The letters are the basis of the design. Since it is a site, have I have made the title parts stand out with the orange color, so the reader knows which part he/she is on the site. I also reproduced these titles with different compositions in order to sign to remain playful.
